ENVIRONMENT

The Dry Valleys in Antarctica are the driest places on earth.
With such low humidity and moisture on this portion of the continent, snow and ice cannot even accumulate, which leaves the valleys as just a dusty expanse of dirt.
Antarctica is, on average, the windiest place on earth. Scientists exploring this southerly landmass have reported wind speeds that have reached up to 200 miles per hour.

ANIMALS

Penguins are often called 'flippered flyers' because of their effortless movement through the water.
This bird is not able to fly; its wings are developed for swimming rather than flying.
